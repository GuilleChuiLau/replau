# Generated Product Image Set

These assets were generated with the built-in image generation tool and then
cropped into 600×600 WebP product-card images.

## Shared visual prompt

Use case: `product-mockup`. Ecommerce restaurant food photography on a warm
light-cream seamless studio background, consistent 45-degree camera angle,
soft diffused key light, appetizing commercial food styling, realistic
textures, centered subject, generous padding, and no people, hands, logos,
labels, packaging text, or watermark.

## Product mapping

- `product-4.webp` through `product-15.webp`: single, double, and triple beef
  hamburgers with the catalog's cheese/bacon combinations.
- `product-16.webp` through `product-21.webp`: fries, onion rings, and chicken
  strips in their small/large or 3/5-piece portions.
- `product-22.webp` and `product-23.webp`: crispy chicken burgers without/with
  cheese.
- `product-24.webp` through `product-26.webp`: single, double, and triple burger
  combos with fries.
- `product-27.webp` and `product-28.webp`: unbranded cold soda and bottled water.
- `product-29.webp` through `product-31.webp`: cheese, bacon, and sauce extras.
- `product-32.webp` through `product-34.webp`: spicy wing portions for 6, 12,
  and 24 pieces.

The images are illustrative menu photography based on product names, not
photographs of the restaurant's exact prepared items. Replace individual files
with real photography later when available, keeping the `product-ID.webp`
filename convention and rerunning `install_generated_product_images.sh`.

## Natural side-order revision

Products 16–19 (small/large fries and onion rings) were regenerated to reduce
the CGI appearance. Fries use irregular lengths, bends, browned edges, visible
salt, and random loose distribution. Onion rings use varied diameters,
imperfect circles, uneven hand-breaded texture, toasted color variation, and
natural crumbs/overlaps. The revised assets remain 600×600 WebP files.
